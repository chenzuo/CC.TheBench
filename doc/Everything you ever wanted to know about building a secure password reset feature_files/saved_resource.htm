                        That's certainly a potential risk Ugo and there are mitigations along the same lines as you'd apply to other brute force attacks (i.e. throttle resets by IP, prolong the request by putting the thread to sleep for a couple of seconds, etc). There's also not a lot of upside for an attacker beyond causing inconvenience to the target.

                        On the flip side, people forget the username or email they signed up to a service with. Assuming you don't want to create an account enumeration risk by disclosing account presence via the browser, email is the only channel you have to privately notify the address that their account doesn't actually exist. I would argue the ability impact of having no feedback to legitimate customers who've simply forgotten which identity they signed up under outweighs the potential risk of brute force when appropriate mitigation is in place.

                                  You can approach this the same way as you would the reset process - don't disclose the existence of the account other than via email. For registration, what this means is that you begin the process with your email address and are then sent an email either with a link to continue the registration process or a notification saying you're already registered.

                                                                                                                                                              Security questions are not really helpful at all, because they are the same one every site and can often been guessed by public information of the user (or by information gathered from other breaking in to other sites).

                                                                                                                                                              I would send a physical letter to the address of user instead of using security questions. You could also charge the user for this. If you are a site that can't afford it or doesn't deal with money, you probably don't need a security question either or should use other means of authentication.
